\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Manages the data exchange in a DEM simulation. 

Basicly, a DEM cycle would involve three rounds of data exchange\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Round one\+: exchange particles as proxies and their corresponding contacts. A particle will be sent as a proxy as its surface exceeds the domain boundary (such that it could potentially contact with particles in other domains). If a particle needs to be sent to other domains as a proxy, its contacts need also to be sent.
\item Round two\+: exchange the contacts of particle proxies back to its original domain, so that the contact forces could be applied onto the original particles.
\item Round three\+: excange particles and their corresponding contacts if particle get out of the domain (i.\+e., the particle turn to belong to another domain after updating its positions at the end of a DEM cycle).
\end{DoxyItemize}

Terminologies\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item \doxylink{classnetdem_1_1_particle}{Particle} proxy or contact proxy\+: \doxylink{classnetdem_1_1_particle}{Particle} belongs to one domain, whereas if it overlaps with other domain, it needs to seed out as a proxy for contact detection and resolution. Thus, proxy means a real object but not belongs to one domain.
\item \doxylink{classnetdem_1_1_particle}{Particle} ghost\+: If a particle is gone (e.\+g., removed or migrated to another domain), it will leave a ghost copy of itself (to prevent the nullptr particle in the contacts). Thus, ghost means a virtual object.
\item Note\+: proxy and ghost may exchange, which has facilitated the programming and computational efficiency. 
\end{DoxyItemize}
